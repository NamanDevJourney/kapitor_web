About Kapitor

Financial Infrastructure
for a Multi-Asset World.

Kapitor was founded to unify fragmented financial systems into one governed platform. Traditional banking, digital assets, yield systems, trade finance, and tokenised assets operate in silos. Kapitor integrates them under one ledger, one wallet, one risk engine, and one compliance framework.

One Unified Ledger

Every transaction, allocation, settlement, and reserve mapping records in a single source of financial truth. No shadow accounting. No off-ledger movements.

One Smart Wallet

A single interface connecting fiat balances, digital assets, yield positions, staking, and payment rails — with full visibility into every position at once.

One Risk Engine

Continuous, real-time risk scoring across every module and user. Dynamic — not quarterly. Automated safeguards, exposure limits, and governance triggers.

One Compliance Framework

Identity verification, AML screening, jurisdiction controls, investment limits, and audit trails — embedded at architecture level, not bolted on.

Regulatory Framework

Compliance Embedded in Design.
Non-Negotiable.

◈
KYC — Know Your Customer

Government-issued ID, biometric verification, address confirmation, sanctions screening, and PEP checks before any user gains access. Four access tiers enforced.

§
KYB — Know Your Business

Corporate registration, beneficial ownership declaration, board authorization, UBO identification, and jurisdictional compliance clearance for all institutional entities.

⊘
AML Monitoring

Continuous AML framework across all modules — before execution, during settlement, and after reconciliation. Transaction pattern analysis, behavioral anomaly detection, velocity monitoring.

⬡
Jurisdiction Controls

Region-based onboarding restrictions, sanctioned country blocking, product eligibility filtering, and tax reporting obligations. No product is universally accessible without regulatory review.

▣
Risk Profiling

Dynamic risk profiles assigned to every user — updated continuously with behavioral change. Governs derivative access, vault eligibility, leverage limits, and product participation.

⇄
Investment Limits

Capital limits enforced by KYC tier, risk profile, jurisdiction, asset category, and product type. Maximum vault allocation, derivative caps, staking ceilings. No unlimited access.
