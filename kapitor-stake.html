Layered Yield Model

Staking as a Capital Layer —
Not a Standalone Tool

Kapitor Stake operates within a layered yield architecture — protocol rewards at the base, optional yield optimization above, risk governance throughout, and unified ledger recording at every step.

L1

Base Network Yield

Direct protocol rewards from ETH, SOL, DOT, and ATOM validators — the foundation of every staking position.

L2

Yield Optimization Layer

Liquid staking derivatives (where eligible) can be deployed into Earn strategies, increasing total yield while maintaining ledger visibility.

L3

Risk Governance Throughout

Exposure caps, slashing risk buffers, and margin stress tests govern every flow — not just at entry, but continuously.

L4

Unified Ledger Mapping

No staking activity exists outside ledger visibility. Every reward, every delegation, every validator change is recorded.

Risk & Governance

Staking Risks Are Disclosed,
Monitored, and Mitigated.

Staking involves validator slashing risk, network downtime exposure, market volatility, and unbonding lock periods. Kapitor mitigates all four through structural controls — not just disclosures.

Validator Diversification

No single validator or cluster receives concentrated delegation. Risk is spread across hundreds of independent operators per network.

Continuous Performance Monitoring

Validator health, uptime, and slashing events are monitored 24/7 with automatic rebalancing triggers if performance degrades.

Capital Buffers

Reserve buffers absorb slashing events before they affect user balances. Buffer adequacy is stress-tested quarterly.

Tier-Based Staking Access

Network access, staking limits, and liquid staking eligibility are determined by compliance tier and risk profile — not open to all by default.

Staking is available based on compliance tier and risk profile selection.
