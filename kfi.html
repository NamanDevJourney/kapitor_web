Digital Issuance

No Manual Ambiguity.
Everything Recorded.

All KFI instruments are digitally issued through a seven-step structured sequence. No instrument can be issued without collateral mapping, risk scoring, governance approval, and ledger allocation. No exceptions.

Unique Instrument ID + Smart Contract

Every instrument receives a unique ID with embedded smart contract linkage — expiry logic, trigger conditions, and transfer eligibility are code-enforced, not manually administered.

No Collateral — No Instrument

Collateral mapping is a prerequisite, not a checkbox. The issuance engine cannot generate an instrument until the reserve allocation is confirmed in the Unified Ledger.

AML + Jurisdictional Screening

Every applicant and counterparty is screened against AML databases and jurisdictional restrictions before issuance approval is granted.

Instantly Auditable

From the moment of issuance, every instrument is audit-retrievable via both the Unified Ledger and blockchain hash record. No reconstruction required.

No manual issuance ambiguity. Everything is recorded and traceable from instrument creation to expiry or settlement.
Blockchain Record Layer

Ledger is Operational Truth.
Blockchain is Verification.

The blockchain layer does not replace the Unified Ledger — it reinforces auditability. Every KFI instrument generates an immutable hash record with timestamp verification, counterparty reference, document checksum, and real-time status updates. Both systems operate together.

Tamper Resistance

Once recorded, the instrument hash cannot be altered. Any document tampering is immediately detectable by comparing the stored checksum against the current document state.

Timestamp Verification

Issuance date, activation events, and status changes are independently verifiable via blockchain timestamp — no reliance on Kapitor's own records for verification.

Cross-Border Trust

Counterparties in different jurisdictions can independently verify instrument authenticity and status without requiring direct access to Kapitor's internal systems.

Regulatory Reporting Support

Blockchain records provide independently verifiable audit trails for regulatory reporting in all supported jurisdictions — reducing reconciliation burden for compliance teams.
